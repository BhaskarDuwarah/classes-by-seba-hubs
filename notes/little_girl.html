The Little Girl - Notes

📖 Summary

Katherine Mansfield’s “The Little Girl” explores the relationship between Kezia, a little girl, and her strict father. Initially, Kezia fears her father, seeing him as a harsh disciplinarian. However, after her mother falls ill and she is left alone with him, Kezia realizes her father’s strictness hides his deep love and care. The story emphasizes that children often misunderstand their parents’ strict behavior as a lack of affection.

📑 Important Questions & Answers

Short Answer Questions (2 Marks)

Q1: Why was Kezia afraid of her father?

A1: Kezia was afraid because her father was very strict and often scolded her.

Q2: What did Kezia make for her father’s birthday?

A2: She made a pin-cushion as a birthday gift for her father.

Q3: Why was Kezia punished?

A3: She was punished because she tore important papers to stuff her pin-cushion.

Q4: What did Kezia realize about her father at the end?

A4: She realized her father was not unkind but tired and worried about his responsibilities.

Q5: How did Kezia’s grandmother try to improve her relationship with her father?

A5: The grandmother encouraged Kezia to talk to her father and make small gifts for him.

Q6: What was the little girl’s reaction when her father came home?

A6: She felt nervous and tried to avoid him as much as possible.

Q7: What happened when Kezia’s mother was hospitalized?

A7: Kezia was left alone with her father and realized he cared deeply for her.

Q8: What is the theme of the story?

A8: The story focuses on parental love and the misunderstandings between parents and children.

Q9: What lesson does “The Little Girl” teach?

A9: It teaches that parents’ strictness often comes from love and concern, not anger.

Q10: What does the phrase “soft and comforting” mean in the story?

A10: It refers to Kezia’s father’s presence when she leaned on him, realizing his love.

Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

Q1: Describe the transformation in Kezia’s feelings towards her father.

A1: At first, Kezia saw her father as a figure of fear because of his strict discipline. But later, when her mother was ill and she had to sleep with her father, she felt his warmth and care. She realized he worked hard for the family and loved her deeply, changing her fear into affection.

Q2: How does the story portray the gap between parents and children?

A2: The story shows that children often misinterpret parents’ strictness as a lack of love. Parents, busy with responsibilities, may fail to show affection, creating a gap. But with communication and understanding, this gap can be bridged.

Q3: Why is the story titled “The Little Girl”? Do you think it’s appropriate?

A3: The title reflects Kezia’s perspective as a young child who misunderstands her father’s behavior. It is appropriate because it focuses on her emotional journey and realization.

Q4: Explain how Mansfield uses the pin-cushion incident to develop the plot.

A4: The pin-cushion incident leads to a conflict between Kezia and her father, reinforcing her fear. This incident contrasts with her later realization of his love, marking her emotional growth.

Q5: Write a diary entry as Kezia after realizing her father’s love.

A5: *“Dear Diary, Tonight I learned my father loves me deeply. I was scared before, but now I know he’s not unkind, just tired from work. I feel safe in his arms.”*

📌 Key Takeaways

  • 👨‍👧 The story highlights the emotional gap between children and busy parents.
  • 💡 It teaches understanding and empathy for parental behavior.
  • ❤️ Love may not always be expressed openly but exists deeply.
