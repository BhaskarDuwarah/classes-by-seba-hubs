Fog - Notes

📖 Exam-ready Summary

The poem “Fog” by Carl Sandburg compares fog to a cat. The fog arrives silently, just like a cat walking on soft paws. It sits quietly, observing the city and harbor, and then moves on without making any noise. The poet uses this metaphor to describe the quiet and mysterious nature of fog. The poem emphasizes the beauty and stillness of natural phenomena and invites readers to observe them closely. Its simple language and vivid imagery make it memorable and thought-provoking.

✨ 10 Medium Answer Questions (3–4 Marks)

Q1 (3–4 Marks): How does the poet personify fog in the poem?

A: The poet personifies fog by comparing it to a cat. Like a cat, the fog arrives silently on “little cat feet,” sits quietly over the city and harbor, and then moves away without making a sound. This personification gives the fog a mysterious, graceful, and almost living quality, making readers view it as a gentle and observant presence.

Q2 (3–4 Marks): What mood is created in the poem “Fog” and how?

A: The poem creates a calm and reflective mood. This is achieved through soft imagery of the fog moving quietly and sitting still like a cat. The absence of noise or disruption in the description gives the reader a sense of peace and the beauty of silence in nature.

Q3 (3–4 Marks): What does the poet mean by “sits looking over harbor and city”?

A: The poet suggests that the fog covers and watches the harbor and city like a cat observing its surroundings. This imagery conveys the idea of the fog as a quiet, watchful entity that temporarily dominates the landscape without causing disturbance.

Q4 (3–4 Marks): How does the metaphor of a cat enhance the meaning of the poem?

A: The cat metaphor makes the fog appear delicate, mysterious, and alive. Cats are known for their silent movements and observant nature. By comparing fog to a cat, the poet emphasizes its quiet arrival, stillness, and departure, which might otherwise go unnoticed.

Q5 (3–4 Marks): How does the brevity of “Fog” contribute to its impact?

A: The poem’s short length mirrors the fleeting presence of fog. Its six concise lines reflect the fog’s sudden arrival and departure. This brevity makes the imagery more powerful, leaving a lasting impression on readers about the quiet beauty of natural phenomena.

Q6 (3–4 Marks): What lesson does “Fog” teach us about observing nature?

A: “Fog” teaches us to appreciate nature’s subtle and silent beauty. It reminds readers that even ordinary events, like fog rolling in, hold charm and mystery. The poem encourages mindfulness and attention to small details in our surroundings.

Q7 (3–4 Marks): Do you think “Fog” is only about a natural phenomenon? Why or why not?

A: No, “Fog” is not just about fog as a weather phenomenon. It also reflects life’s uncertainties and moments of pause. Like fog, challenges or periods of confusion can appear silently, stay briefly, and then pass, teaching us patience and observation.

Q8 (3–4 Marks): How does the poet’s use of free verse add to the poem’s meaning?

A: The free verse mirrors the fog’s natural, unstructured movement. Without rhyme or strict meter, the poem flows gently, like fog spreading over a landscape. This form enhances the sense of freedom and unpredictability in nature.

Q9 (3–4 Marks): Why does the poet describe the fog’s movement as “on little cat feet”?

A: This phrase highlights the fog’s silent and delicate arrival. Like a cat, it moves unnoticed, covering its surroundings softly without disruption. The image gives the fog a gentle and graceful quality.

Q10 (3–4 Marks): Explain the theme of transience in “Fog.”

A: The theme of transience is shown as the fog comes quietly, lingers for a while, and leaves without warning. This reflects the temporary nature of many experiences in life. It suggests that beauty and challenges alike are fleeting, teaching readers to appreciate the present moment.

📜 6 Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

Q1 (5 Marks): Discuss the central idea of “Fog” and how Carl Sandburg uses imagery to convey it.

A: The central idea of “Fog” is the quiet and mysterious arrival of natural events and their transient nature. Sandburg uses the metaphor of a cat to describe fog. Just like a cat enters softly on “little cat feet,” the fog comes unnoticed, sits over the city and harbor silently, and departs without a sound. The imagery creates a visual and sensory picture of the fog’s movement, emphasizing its grace and calmness. The poem teaches us to notice and appreciate such subtle phenomena in nature and in life.

Q2 (5 Marks): Explain how the poet personifies fog in the poem. What effect does this have on readers?

A: The poet personifies fog by comparing it to a cat. This comparison gives the fog a life-like quality. It comes softly, pauses quietly, and leaves as silently as it arrived. The cat metaphor suggests a sense of independence and mystery, making readers view fog as more than just a weather condition. It encourages people to observe nature with more sensitivity and admire its delicate movements. The effect is a heightened awareness of the beauty and grace in natural processes.

Q3 (5 Marks): What lesson about life can we learn from the poem “Fog”?

A: The poem teaches us about the impermanence of situations in life. Just as fog comes and goes without warning, life’s challenges and beautiful moments are also temporary. This encourages readers to accept change and appreciate the present. The poem reminds us that even quiet, unnoticed events can have beauty and significance if we pause to observe them. It promotes mindfulness and an appreciation for nature’s simplicity.

Q4 (5 Marks): How does the structure of the poem reflect the nature of fog?

A: The poem’s brief, free-verse structure mirrors the fog’s short and quiet presence. Its six lines, with no rhyme scheme or meter, flow smoothly like fog drifting over a city. This simplicity reflects the effortless movement of fog and highlights its subtle yet powerful impact. The lack of complexity in structure also allows the reader to focus entirely on the imagery and metaphor, enhancing the poem’s message about observing life’s quiet details.

Q5 (5 Marks): How does “Fog” show the beauty of natural phenomena?

A: “Fog” shows beauty in nature’s simplicity. The poet compares fog to a cat to capture its soft, silent, and graceful movements. This metaphor makes readers see fog as a living presence, adding charm to the landscape. The poem reflects that beauty does not always come in loud or grand forms but can exist in quiet, fleeting moments like fog covering a city. It inspires readers to appreciate such unnoticed yet magical aspects of the natural world.

Q6 (5 Marks): Do you agree that “Fog” is a metaphorical poem? Why or why not?

A: Yes, “Fog” is a metaphorical poem. The poet compares fog to a cat, giving it human-like traits of movement and observation. This metaphor extends beyond describing fog; it also reflects life’s uncertainties and the temporary nature of experiences. Just like fog, moments of confusion, calmness, or beauty enter our lives quietly, stay briefly, and move on. The metaphor encourages readers to accept life’s transitions gracefully and notice the small wonders around them.
