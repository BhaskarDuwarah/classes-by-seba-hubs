‚úèÔ∏è 18 One Mark Questions

Q1: What is reproduction?

A: The biological process by which new individuals of the same species are produced.

Q2: Name the method of reproduction in Amoeba.

A: Binary fission.

Q3: Name one organism that reproduces by budding.

A: Hydra.

Q4: What is pollination?

A: Transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma of a flower.

Q5: What is fertilization?

A: Fusion of male and female gametes to form a zygote.

Q6: Name the male gamete in humans.

A: Sperm.

Q7: Name the female gamete in humans.

A: Ovum (egg).

Q8: What is the function of the fallopian tube in the female reproductive system?

A: It is the site where fertilization occurs.

Q9: What type of reproduction occurs in yeast?

A: Budding.

Q10: Define vegetative propagation.

A: A type of asexual reproduction where new plants grow from parts like roots, stems, or leaves.

Q11: Give an example of a plant which reproduces by leaf.

A: Bryophyllum.

Q12: What is the role of the placenta in human reproduction?

A: It supplies nutrients and oxygen to the embryo and removes waste.

Q13: Which part of the flower develops into a fruit?

A: Ovary.

Q14: Name one sexually transmitted disease (STD) caused by a virus.

A: AIDS (caused by HIV).

Q15: What is the chromosome number in human gametes?

A: 23 (haploid).

Q16: Name the reproductive organ in males that produces sperm.

A: Testes.

Q17: Which hormone regulates the menstrual cycle in females?

A: Estrogen and progesterone.

Q18: What is the duration of the menstrual cycle in humans?

A: Approximately 28 days.

Q6: Explain the function of the placenta in humans.

A: The placenta allows exchange of nutrients, gases, and wastes between mother and embryo.

Q7: What is the difference between external and internal fertilization?

A: External fertilization occurs outside the body (e.g., frog), internal fertilization occurs inside the body (e.g., humans).

Q8: Mention two advantages of sexual reproduction.

A: (i) Introduces variation in species. (ii) Helps in evolution and adaptation.

Q9: What is the significance of DNA copying in reproduction?

A: DNA copying ensures transfer of genetic information and introduces variations essential for evolution.

Q13: What is fragmentation? Give one example.

A: Breaking of an organism into pieces which grow into new organisms. Example: Spirogyra.

Q14: Explain why fertilization is essential for sexual reproduction.

‚ú® 10 Medium Answer Questions (3‚Äì4 Marks)

Q1: Explain the process of fertilization in flowering plants with a diagram.

A: Fertilization occurs when a pollen grain reaches the stigma, germinates, and the pollen tube grows through the style to reach the ovary. The male gamete fuses with the egg cell to form a zygote. (Diagram of fertilization in flowers recommended.)

Q2: Describe the human male reproductive system with a neat labelled diagram.

A: It consists of testes (produce sperm and testosterone), vas deferens (transports sperm), seminal vesicles (produce seminal fluid), urethra (conveys sperm and urine). (Diagram of male reproductive system required.)

Q3: Write differences between binary fission, multiple fission, and budding with examples.

A: - Binary fission: One cell splits into two (e.g., Amoeba). - Multiple fission: One cell splits into many daughter cells (e.g., Plasmodium). - Budding: A new organism grows from a bud (e.g., Hydra).

Q4: Explain vegetative propagation with two examples. Why is it useful?

A: It is reproduction using vegetative parts (like stems or leaves). Examples: - Potato (stem tuber) - Bryophyllum (leaf buds). Useful because it produces clones and allows propagation without seeds.

Q5: Describe the role of placenta during pregnancy.

A: The placenta connects mother and embryo. It allows exchange of nutrients, oxygen, and wastes. It also produces hormones for maintaining pregnancy.

Q6: How does DNA copying during reproduction contribute to variations? Why are variations important?

A: DNA copying is not always perfect and introduces small changes. Variations help organisms survive changes in the environment and promote evolution.

Q7: Draw and label the human female reproductive system. Briefly explain its parts.

A: The system includes ovaries (produce eggs), fallopian tubes (site of fertilization), uterus (embryo development), vagina (birth canal). (Diagram of female reproductive system recommended.)

Q8: Explain cross-pollination and self-pollination with examples. Which is better and why?

A: - Self-pollination: Pollen transferred within same flower (e.g., peas). - Cross-pollination: Pollen transferred between flowers of same species (e.g., maize). Cross-pollination is better as it promotes genetic variation.

Q9: What are sexually transmitted diseases (STDs)? Name two bacterial and two viral STDs.

A: STDs are infections transmitted through sexual contact. - Bacterial: Syphilis, Gonorrhea. - Viral: AIDS (HIV), Genital herpes.

Q10: How is the embryo protected inside the mother‚Äôs womb?

A: The embryo is embedded in the uterine wall and surrounded by amniotic fluid, which cushions it against shocks and protects from desiccation and infections.

üåü 5 Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

Q1: Explain the process of reproduction in flowering plants with a neat labelled diagram.

A: The reproductive parts of a flower are the stamen (male) and carpel (female). The process includes: 1. Pollination ‚Äì Transfer of pollen grains from anther to stigma. 2. Germination ‚Äì Pollen grain germinates and forms a pollen tube. 3. Fertilization ‚Äì Male gametes fuse with egg cell in ovule. 4. Seed and Fruit Formation ‚Äì Fertilized ovule develops into seed; ovary develops into fruit. (Diagram of a flower showing anther, stigma, pollen tube recommended.)

Q2: Describe the human male and female reproductive systems with labelled diagrams. Explain how they function together for reproduction.

A: - Male reproductive system: Testes (produce sperm), vas deferens (transport), seminal vesicles, urethra. - Female reproductive system: Ovaries (produce ova), fallopian tubes (site of fertilization), uterus (embryo development). - During reproduction, sperm is deposited in vagina, travels to fallopian tube, fertilizes ovum forming zygote which implants in uterus. (Diagrams of both systems required.)

Q3: What is asexual reproduction? Describe five types with examples.

A: Asexual reproduction involves a single parent and no gamete fusion. Types: 1. Binary Fission (Amoeba) 2. Multiple Fission (Plasmodium) 3. Budding (Hydra) 4. Fragmentation (Spirogyra) 5. Vegetative Propagation (Potato, Bryophyllum). (Draw binary fission in Amoeba and budding in Hydra for clarity.)

Q4: Explain the menstrual cycle in humans. Why is it important for reproduction?

A: Menstrual cycle is a 28-day cycle involving: - Follicle growth and ovum release (ovulation around day 14). - Thickening of uterine lining for possible implantation. - If fertilization fails, the lining sheds (menstruation). Importance: Prepares uterus for pregnancy each month.

Q5: What are sexually transmitted diseases (STDs)? Explain their causes, symptoms, prevention, and why they are a threat to reproductive health.

A: STDs are infections spread by sexual contact. - Causes: Bacteria (syphilis, gonorrhea), viruses (HIV/AIDS, herpes). - Symptoms: Sores, discharge, infertility in severe cases. - Prevention: Safe sex practices, awareness, early diagnosis. Importance: They can damage reproductive organs and prevent conception.
