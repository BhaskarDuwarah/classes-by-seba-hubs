Q1 (2 Marks): Why does carbon form covalent bonds?

A: Carbon has 4 valence electrons and needs 4 more to complete its octet. It cannot lose or gain 4 electrons easily, so it shares electrons forming covalent bonds.

Q2 (2 Marks): What is catenation? Name two elements that exhibit this property.

A: Catenation is the ability of an element to form bonds with other atoms of the same element to form long chains. Example: Carbon and Silicon.

Q3 (2 Marks): Give the structural formula of ethanol and write its functional group.

A: CH3CH2OH; Functional group: ‚ÄìOH (hydroxyl group)

Q4 (2 Marks): Differentiate between saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons.

A: Saturated hydrocarbons have single bonds only (alkanes); unsaturated hydrocarbons have double or triple bonds (alkenes and alkynes).

A: 2C2H5OH + 2Na ‚Üí 2C2H5ONa + H2‚Üë

Q6 (2 Marks): What happens when ethanol is oxidized? Write the reaction.

A: Ethanol is oxidized to ethanoic acid. C2H5OH + O ‚Üí CH3COOH + H2O

Q7 (2 Marks): Why are covalent compounds poor conductors of electricity?

A: Covalent compounds do not have free electrons or ions to carry electric current.

Q8 (2 Marks): Write two uses of ethanol in daily life.

A: (i) Used as a solvent in medicines and perfumes.
(ii) Used as fuel (in spirit lamps).

Q9 (2 Marks): What is the difference between soap and detergent?

A: Soap is made from natural fats/oils and alkali; detergents are synthetic and work even in hard water.

Q10 (2 Marks): What is hydrogenation? Give one example.

A: Hydrogenation is the addition of hydrogen to unsaturated hydrocarbons in the presence of a catalyst. Example: C2H4 + H2 ‚Üí C2H6 (using Ni catalyst)

Q12 (2 Marks): Why does soap not work well in hard water?

A: Hard water contains Ca2+ and Mg2+ ions which react with soap to form insoluble scum.

Q13 (2 Marks): Write the general formula of alkenes. Give an example.

A: General formula: CnH2n; Example: Ethene (C2H4).

Q14 (2 Marks): Write the reaction of saponification.

A: Fat + NaOH ‚Üí Soap + Glycerol

Q15 (2 Marks): What are homologous series? Give an example.

A: Series of compounds with the same functional group differing by ‚ÄìCH2‚Äì. Example: Methane (CH4), Ethane (C2H6).

Q1: What is the difference between saturated and unsaturated hydrocarbons? Give one example of each.

Q2: What is ethanol? Write two physical properties of ethanol.

Q5: Why do ionic compounds conduct electricity in molten state but not in solid state?

A: In solid state, ions are fixed. In molten state, ions are free to move and conduct electricity.

A: Soap: Made from fats and oils, does not work well in hard water (Example: Sodium stearate). Detergent: Made from petrochemicals, works in hard water (Example: Sodium lauryl sulfate).

Q7: Explain why carbon forms covalent bonds.

A: Carbon has 4 valence electrons; gaining or losing 4 electrons is energetically unfavorable. So, it shares electrons to form covalent bonds.

A: Graphite has delocalized electrons that conduct electricity. Diamond has no free electrons.
