‚úèÔ∏è 15 Short Answer Questions (2 Marks)

Q1: Differentiate between sensory and motor neurons.

A: Sensory neurons carry impulses from sense organs to CNS; motor neurons carry impulses from CNS to effectors.

Q2: What is a reflex arc? Draw a labeled diagram.

A: Reflex arc is the pathway of nerve impulse during a reflex action. (Diagram required)

Q3: Name two involuntary actions and the parts of the brain controlling them.

A: Breathing (medulla), heartbeat (medulla).

Q4: Write two differences between endocrine and nervous system.

A: Endocrine uses hormones, slower response; nervous uses impulses, faster response.

Q5: What are tropic movements? Name two examples in plants.

A: Growth movements due to stimuli. Examples: Phototropism, geotropism.

Q6: Why is pituitary gland called the master gland?

A: Because it controls other endocrine glands by secreting tropic hormones.

Q7: What is the function of adrenaline hormone?

A: Prepares body for fight or flight by increasing heart rate and blood flow.

Q8: Why is coordination necessary in living organisms?

A: To maintain proper functioning and survival by synchronizing body activities.

Q9: Explain why auxins are called growth hormones in plants.

A: Auxins stimulate cell elongation, root initiation, and phototropism.

Q10: Name two hormones secreted by pancreas and their functions.

A: Insulin (lowers blood glucose), Glucagon (raises blood glucose).

Q11: What is synaptic transmission?

A: Transfer of nerve impulse from one neuron to another through synapse using neurotransmitters.

Q12: Differentiate between phototropism and geotropism.

A: Phototropism is growth towards light; geotropism is growth towards gravity.

Q13: State two functions of medulla oblongata.

A: Controls involuntary actions like breathing and heartbeat.

Q14: What are effectors? Give examples.

A: Effectors are muscles or glands that respond to nerve signals. Examples: biceps muscle, salivary glands.

Q15: Why are electrical impulses preferred in the nervous system?

A: Because they are faster and allow quick responses to stimuli.

‚úèÔ∏è 10 Medium Answer Questions (3‚Äì4 Marks)

Q1: Explain the structure and functions of a neuron with a labeled diagram.

A: Neurons have dendrites (receive signals), cell body (processes signals), axon (transmits signals). Diagram required for full marks.

Q2: Describe the mechanism of reflex action with the help of a diagram.

A: Reflex action is an involuntary response. Pathway: receptor ‚Üí sensory neuron ‚Üí spinal cord ‚Üí motor neuron ‚Üí effector. Diagram required.

Q3: Compare nervous control and hormonal control in humans (any 4 points).

A: Nervous: fast, electrical impulses, short-lived, specific organs. Hormonal: slow, chemical messengers, long-lasting, whole body effects.

Q4: What are plant hormones? Write functions of auxins and gibberellins.

A: Plant hormones are chemicals regulating growth. Auxins: cell elongation, phototropism. Gibberellins: stem elongation, seed germination.

Q5: Explain the human brain's main parts and their functions.

A: Forebrain: intelligence, emotions. Midbrain: vision and hearing reflexes. Hindbrain: balance (cerebellum), involuntary actions (medulla).

Q6: How does adrenaline prepare the body for emergency situations?

A: Increases heartbeat, respiration, blood flow to muscles, and pupil dilation for "fight or flight" response.

Q7: What is phototropism? Explain with an experiment using coleoptile tips.

A: Growth towards light due to auxin redistribution. Experiment: Tip removal prevents bending; tip reattached restores bending towards light.

Q8: Draw and explain the structure of a synapse.

A: Synapse allows transmission between neurons via neurotransmitters. Diagram must show presynaptic neuron, synaptic cleft, postsynaptic neuron.

Q9: Differentiate between voluntary, involuntary, and reflex actions with examples.

A: Voluntary: under control (writing). Involuntary: not under control (heartbeat). Reflex: sudden automatic action (hand withdrawal from hot object).

Q10: What is geotropism? Explain with an experiment using germinating seeds.

A: Growth towards gravity. Experiment: Place germinating seeds horizontally; roots grow down (positive geotropism), shoots grow up (negative geotropism).

üìù 5 Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

Q1: Explain the human brain's structure with a neat labeled diagram. Mention the functions of each part.

A: - **Forebrain:** Intelligence, memory, emotions, voluntary actions. - **Midbrain:** Controls reflex movements of head and neck in response to visual and auditory stimuli. - **Hindbrain:** Cerebellum (balance & posture), Medulla oblongata (involuntary actions like heartbeat and breathing). - Include diagram showing forebrain, midbrain, and hindbrain.

Q2: Describe the structure and functioning of a neuron. Explain how nerve impulses are transmitted across a synapse.

A: - **Neuron Structure:** Dendrites (receive), Cell body (processes), Axon (transmits). - **Synapse Transmission:** Neurotransmitters released from axon terminals cross synaptic cleft and bind to receptors on the next neuron, propagating the signal. - Diagram required for full marks.

Q3: Explain phototropism and geotropism in plants with the help of experiments and diagrams.

A: - **Phototropism:** Growth of shoots towards light due to auxin redistribution. - **Geotropism:** Roots grow downward (positive), shoots grow upward (negative). - Experiments: Coleoptile tip experiment for phototropism; germinating seed placed horizontally for geotropism. - Diagrams required.

Q4: What is the reflex action? Explain the pathway of a reflex arc with a labeled diagram.

A: - Reflex action: Automatic, quick response to a stimulus. - **Reflex arc:** Receptor ‚Üí Sensory neuron ‚Üí Spinal cord ‚Üí Motor neuron ‚Üí Effector. - Example: Pulling hand away from a hot object. - Include a neat diagram showing the pathway.

Q5: Differentiate between the nervous system and endocrine system on the basis of: (i) Nature of message (ii) Speed of response (iii) Duration of response (iv) Mode of transmission (v) Example of action.

A: - Nature: Nervous (electrical), Endocrine (chemical hormones).
- Speed: Nervous (fast), Endocrine (slow).
- Duration: Nervous (short-lived), Endocrine (long-lasting).
- Mode: Nervous (neurons), Endocrine (blood).
- Example: Nervous (reflex), Endocrine (adrenaline release).
