Life Processes - Notes

üìñ Summary

Life processes are the essential biological activities performed by living organisms to sustain life, such as nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion. Plants (autotrophs) prepare their own food through photosynthesis, while animals (heterotrophs) depend on others. The circulatory system distributes nutrients and oxygen, and the excretory system removes waste products. Together, these processes maintain homeostasis and support life.

Q1: Name the pigment responsible for photosynthesis.

Q2: Which gas is released during photosynthesis?

A: Oxygen (O2).

Q3: What is the functional unit of the kidney?

Q5: Which blood cells help in clotting?

A: Carbon dioxide, water, and ATP (energy).

A: Alveoli in the lungs.

Q8: Which plant tissue transports water?

A: Xylem.

Q9: Name the enzyme in saliva that breaks down starch.

A: Salivary amylase (ptyalin).

Q10: What is the main excretory product in humans?

A: Urea.

Q11: Name the blood vessel carrying oxygenated blood from lungs to heart.

A: Pulmonary vein.

A: Plasma.

Q14: What is transpiration?

A: Loss of water vapor from aerial parts of a plant.

Q15: Name the valve between left atrium and ventricle.

A: Bicuspid (mitral) valve.

Q16: What is the normal pH of human blood?

A: Around 7.4.

Q17: Name the structure responsible for opening and closing stomata.

Q18: What is lymph?

A: A clear fluid involved in immune response and transportation of substances.

‚úèÔ∏è 15 Short Answer Questions (2 Marks)

Q1: Differentiate between autotrophic and heterotrophic nutrition.

A: Autotrophs make their food (e.g., plants); heterotrophs depend on others (e.g., humans).

Q2: Why is small intestine longer in herbivores?

A: Herbivores digest cellulose-rich food which needs more time for absorption.

Q3: Write two differences between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.

A: Aerobic uses oxygen, produces more energy; anaerobic doesn‚Äôt use oxygen, produces less energy.

Q4: What is double circulation? Why is it important?

A: Blood flows twice through the heart per cycle. It separates oxygenated and deoxygenated blood.

Q5: State two functions of stomata.

A: Gas exchange and water vapor release (transpiration).

Q6: Define transpiration and give its importance.

A: Loss of water vapor; helps in cooling and upward water movement.

Q7: Why are lungs spongy?

A: Due to alveoli which increase surface area for gas exchange.

Q8: What is the role of bile in digestion?

A: Emulsifies fats and makes them easier to digest by lipase.

Q9: Why does the left ventricle have thicker walls?

A: It pumps blood to the whole body requiring higher pressure.

Q10: Explain the function of nephron.

A: Filters blood and removes waste products to form urine.

Q11: Name two substances transported by xylem and phloem each.

A: Xylem: Water, minerals; Phloem: Sugars, amino acids.

Q12: Differentiate between arteries and veins (any two points).

A: Arteries carry blood away from heart, veins towards; arteries have thick walls, veins thin.

Q13: What is lymph and its two functions?

A: Lymph transports nutrients and fights infections.

Q14: Write two differences between photosynthesis and respiration.

A: Photosynthesis stores energy; respiration releases energy.

Q15: State two adaptations in plants for photosynthesis.

A: Broad leaves (large surface area); chloroplasts near surface cells.
