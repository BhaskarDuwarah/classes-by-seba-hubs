This chapter focuses on the behavior of light when it travels from one medium to another. It explains the laws of reflection and refraction, image formation by spherical mirrors and lenses, and their applications in daily life.

Key concepts include:

  • Reflection: bouncing back of light from a surface.
  • Refraction: bending of light when it passes from one medium to another due to a change in speed.
  • Laws of Reflection and Refraction.
  • Image formation by concave/convex mirrors and lenses.
  • Applications like rear-view mirrors, magnifying glasses, etc.

Q1: State the laws of reflection.

A: (i) The angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection. (ii) Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in the same plane.

Q2: What is meant by the refractive index?

A: Refractive index = Speed of light in vacuum / Speed of light in medium.

Q3: Name the type of mirror used as a rear-view mirror in vehicles.

A: Convex mirror.

Q4: Write the mirror formula.

A: 1/f = 1/v + 1/u

Q5: What is the SI unit of power of a lens?

A: Dioptre (D).

Q6: Define focal length.

A: The distance between the pole and the focus of a mirror/lens.

Q7: What is a concave lens?

A: A lens thinner at the center and thicker at the edges.

Q8: What type of image is formed by a plane mirror?

A: Virtual, erect, and same size as the object.

Q9: Define angle of incidence.

A: The angle between the incident ray and the normal.

Q10: What is total internal reflection?

A: Reflection of light back into a denser medium when the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle.

Q13: What happens when light travels from air to glass?

A: It bends towards the normal (refraction).

Q14: What type of lens is used to correct myopia?

A: Concave lens.

Q15: Write the relation between radius of curvature (R) and focal length (f).

A: f = R/2

Q16: Define power of a lens.

A: The ability of a lens to converge or diverge light, reciprocal of focal length (P=100/f).

Q17: What happens to the speed of light when it enters a denser medium?

A: Speed decreases.

Q18: What is the nature of the image formed by a convex mirror?

A: Virtual, erect, and diminished.

Q2: Derive the Mirror Formula (Step by Step)

üìù Question:

Derive the mirror formula for a concave mirror using ray diagrams and geometry.

‚úÖ Answer:

Step 1: Consider a concave mirror with pole \( P \), center of curvature \( C \), and principal focus \( F \).

Let an object \( AB \) be placed at a distance \( u \) from the pole \( P \). The image \( A'B' \) is formed at a distance \( v \) from \( P \).

Step 2: Draw a ray diagram:

  • Ray parallel to the principal axis reflects through \( F \).
  • Ray passing through \( C \) reflects back on itself.

Step 3: From the diagram, consider two similar triangles:

‚ñ≥A'B'F and ‚ñ≥ABF

By similarity, we get:

\(\frac{A'B'}{AB} = \frac{A'F}{AF}\)

Step 4: Using sign convention and geometry:

A'F = v - f

AF = u - f

So:

\(\frac{v'}{u} = \frac{v - f}{u - f}\)

Step 5: Rearrange the terms and simplify:

\(\frac{v}{u} = \frac{v - f}{u - f}\)

Cross-multiplying gives:

v(u - f) = u(v - f)

vu - vf = vu - uf

Cancel \( vu \) from both sides:

-vf = -uf

Simplify:

vuf = uvf

Step 6: Divide through by \( uvf \):

\(\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}\)

Final Answer: The mirror formula is: \(\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{u} + \frac{1}{v}\)
