Q1 (1 Mark): What is a chemical reaction?

A: A process in which one or more substances are converted into new substances with different properties.

Q2 (1 Mark): Define a balanced chemical equation.

A: A chemical equation where the number of atoms of each element is the same on both sides of the equation.

Q3 (1 Mark): Give an example of a combination reaction.

A: CaO + H2O ‚Üí Ca(OH)2

Q4 (1 Mark): Write the chemical formula of quicklime.

A: CaO

Q5 (1 Mark): What is corrosion?

A: Gradual destruction of metals by reaction with air, water, or chemicals (e.g., rusting of iron).

Q6 (1 Mark): Name the gas evolved when zinc reacts with dilute sulphuric acid.

A: Hydrogen gas (H2).

Q7 (1 Mark): What type of reaction is photosynthesis?

A: Endothermic reaction.

Q8 (1 Mark): Write an example of a decomposition reaction.

A: 2H2O ‚Üí 2H2 + O2

Q9 (1 Mark): What is rancidity?

A: The spoilage of food containing fats and oils due to oxidation, producing a bad smell and taste.

Q10 (1 Mark): Name a reducing agent used in the extraction of metals.

A: Carbon (C).

Q11 (1 Mark): What does the symbol (aq) in a chemical equation indicate?

A: It means the substance is in aqueous solution (dissolved in water).

Q12 (1 Mark): Name the process of adding oxygen to a substance.

A: Oxidation.

Q13 (1 Mark): Write the chemical formula of slaked lime.

A: Ca(OH)2

Q14 (1 Mark): Give an example of a displacement reaction.

A: Zn + CuSO4 ‚Üí ZnSO4 + Cu

Q15 (1 Mark): What is an endothermic reaction?

A: A reaction that absorbs heat energy from its surroundings.

Q16 (1 Mark): What is an exothermic reaction?

A: A reaction that releases heat energy into its surroundings.

Q17 (1 Mark): Write the chemical equation for rusting of iron.

A: 4Fe + 3O2 + 6H2O ‚Üí 4Fe(OH)3

Q18 (1 Mark): What type of reaction is respiration?

A: Exothermic reaction.

Q1 (2 Marks): What is the difference between a balanced and an unbalanced chemical equation? Give one example each.

A: In a balanced equation, the number of atoms of each element is the same on both sides. Example: 2H2 + O2 ‚Üí 2H2O (Balanced). In an unbalanced equation, this is not true. Example: H2 + O2 ‚Üí H2O (Unbalanced).

Q2 (2 Marks): What is a decomposition reaction? Write one example.

A: A reaction where a single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances. Example: 2H2O ‚Üí 2H2 + O2 (by electrolysis).

Q3 (2 Marks): Define oxidation and reduction with examples.

A: Oxidation: Gain of oxygen or loss of hydrogen. Example: Mg + O2 ‚Üí MgO. Reduction: Loss of oxygen or gain of hydrogen. Example: CuO + H2 ‚Üí Cu + H2O.

Q4 (2 Marks): What is the chemical formula for rust? How is it formed?

A: The formula for rust is Fe2O3¬∑xH2O. It is formed when iron reacts with oxygen and water in the air.

Q5 (2 Marks): What is rancidity? How can it be prevented?

A: Rancidity is the oxidation of fats and oils, producing bad odor and taste. It can be prevented by refrigeration, using antioxidants, or storing food in airtight containers.

Q6 (2 Marks): Explain displacement reaction with an example.

A: A reaction where a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound. Example: Zn + CuSO4 ‚Üí ZnSO4 + Cu.

Q7 (2 Marks): What is the difference between exothermic and endothermic reactions? Give one example each.

A: Exothermic: Heat is released (e.g., CH4 + 2O2 ‚Üí CO2 + 2H2O + Heat). Endothermic: Heat is absorbed (e.g., Photosynthesis).

Q8 (2 Marks): Balance the following chemical equation: Fe + H2O ‚Üí Fe3O4 + H2

A: 3Fe + 4H2O ‚Üí Fe3O4 + 4H2

Q9 (2 Marks): Why is respiration considered an exothermic reaction?

A: Respiration releases energy. Glucose is broken down with oxygen to produce CO2, water, and energy. Reaction: C6H12O6 + 6O2 ‚Üí 6CO2 + 6H2O + Energy.

Q10 (2 Marks): What happens when silver chloride is exposed to sunlight? Write the reaction.

A: It turns grey due to decomposition. Reaction: 2AgCl ‚Üí 2Ag + Cl2 (in sunlight).

Q11 (2 Marks): What are the two main types of chemical reactions involved in corrosion?

A: Oxidation of metal and formation of hydrated metal oxides.

Q12 (2 Marks): Give an example of a double displacement reaction.

A: Na2SO4 + BaCl2 ‚Üí BaSO4 + 2NaCl

Q13 (2 Marks): What is the role of a catalyst in a chemical reaction?

A: A catalyst speeds up a chemical reaction without being consumed. Example: MnO2 acts as a catalyst in the decomposition of KClO3.

Q14 (2 Marks): What type of reaction is: CaCO3 ‚Üí CaO + CO2?

A: Decomposition reaction.

Q15 (2 Marks): State the law of conservation of mass with an example.

A: Mass is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. Example: H2 + O2 ‚Üí H2O (mass of reactants = mass of products).

Q1 (3‚Äì4 Marks): Explain with examples the difference between a combination and a decomposition reaction.

A: In a combination reaction, two or more reactants combine to form a single product. Example: CaO + H2O ‚Üí Ca(OH)2. In a decomposition reaction, a single compound breaks down into two or more simpler substances. Example: 2H2O ‚Üí 2H2 + O2 (electrolysis).

Q2 (3‚Äì4 Marks): Balance the following equation and identify the type of reaction: Fe + H2O ‚Üí Fe3O4 + H2

A: Balanced Equation: 3Fe + 4H2O ‚Üí Fe3O4 + 4H2. This is a redox reaction as Fe is oxidized and H2O is reduced.

Q3 (3‚Äì4 Marks): What is corrosion? Explain with an example. How can it be prevented?

A: Corrosion is the slow destruction of metals due to reaction with air, water, or chemicals. Example: Iron rusts forming Fe2O3¬∑xH2O. Prevention: Painting, oiling, galvanization, or using corrosion-resistant alloys like stainless steel.

Q4 (3‚Äì4 Marks): What happens when: (a) Lead nitrate is heated (b) Silver chloride is exposed to sunlight?

A: (a) 2Pb(NO3)2 ‚Üí 2PbO + 4NO2 + O2 (thermal decomposition). (b) 2AgCl ‚Üí 2Ag + Cl2 (photo decomposition).

Q5 (3‚Äì4 Marks): Explain oxidation and reduction with examples. Are they complementary processes?

A: Oxidation is the gain of oxygen or loss of hydrogen (Mg + O2 ‚Üí MgO). Reduction is the loss of oxygen or gain of hydrogen (CuO + H2 ‚Üí Cu + H2O). Yes, they are complementary and occur simultaneously in redox reactions.

Q6 (3‚Äì4 Marks): Differentiate between displacement and double displacement reactions with examples.

A: Displacement: A more reactive element displaces a less reactive one (Zn + CuSO4 ‚Üí ZnSO4 + Cu). Double Displacement: Exchange of ions between two compounds (Na2SO4 + BaCl2 ‚Üí BaSO4 + 2NaCl).

Q7 (3‚Äì4 Marks): Write two examples each of exothermic and endothermic reactions.

A: Exothermic: (i) C + O2 ‚Üí CO2 + heat (ii) CH4 + 2O2 ‚Üí CO2 + 2H2O + heat Endothermic: (i) Photosynthesis: 6CO2 + 6H2O ‚Üí C6H12O6 + O2 (ii) Thermal decomposition of CaCO3: CaCO3 ‚Üí CaO + CO2

Q8 (3‚Äì4 Marks): What are the causes of rancidity? How can it be prevented?

A: Rancidity is caused by oxidation of oils and fats, resulting in bad odor and taste. Prevention: Use antioxidants (like BHT), keep food in airtight containers, or refrigerate.

Q9 (3‚Äì4 Marks): Explain the role of light in a chemical reaction with an example.

A: Light acts as energy to start photo decomposition reactions. Example: 2AgCl ‚Üí 2Ag + Cl2 (in sunlight). Here, sunlight breaks down silver chloride into silver and chlorine.

Q10 (3‚Äì4 Marks): Why should a chemical equation be balanced? Illustrate with an example.

A: A chemical equation should be balanced to obey the law of conservation of mass. Example: Unbalanced ‚Äì H2 + O2 ‚Üí H2O. Balanced ‚Äì 2H2 + O2 ‚Üí 2H2O.
