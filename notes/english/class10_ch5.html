Q1 (2 Marks): Why did Mendeleev leave gaps in his periodic table?

A: Mendeleev left gaps in his table because he predicted the existence and properties of undiscovered elements like gallium and germanium.

Q2 (2 Marks): State two limitations of Mendeleev‚Äôs periodic table.

A: 1. Position of isotopes was not explained.
2. Some elements were placed in incorrect order based on atomic masses (e.g., cobalt and nickel).

Q3 (2 Marks): Define modern periodic law and explain its significance.

A: Modern periodic law states: ‚ÄúProperties of elements are periodic functions of their atomic numbers.‚Äù It corrected flaws of Mendeleev‚Äôs table and arranged elements logically.

Q4 (2 Marks): What are groups and periods in the periodic table?

A: Groups are vertical columns (18 total), and periods are horizontal rows (7 total) in the modern periodic table.

Q5 (2 Marks): Why does atomic size decrease across a period?

A: Atomic size decreases because nuclear charge increases, pulling electrons closer to the nucleus.

Q6 (2 Marks): Why does atomic size increase down a group?

A: Atomic size increases because new electron shells are added down the group, increasing the distance between nucleus and outermost electrons.

Q7 (2 Marks): What is meant by periodicity? Name two periodic properties.

A: Periodicity is the repetition of properties of elements at regular intervals in the periodic table. Examples: Atomic radius and electronegativity.

Q8 (2 Marks): Write two reasons why noble gases are placed in Group 18.

A: 1. They have completely filled valence shells.
2. They are chemically inert (do not react easily).

Q9 (2 Marks): Define valency. How does it vary across a period?

A: Valency is the combining capacity of an element. Across a period, valency first increases from 1 to 4, then decreases back to 0 in noble gases.

Q10 (2 Marks): State two differences between Mendeleev‚Äôs and Modern periodic tables.

A: 1. Mendeleev: Based on atomic mass; Modern: Based on atomic number.
2. Mendeleev‚Äôs table had gaps for undiscovered elements; Modern table is complete.

Q11 (2 Marks): Why do metallic characters increase down a group?

A: Metallic character increases because atoms can lose electrons more easily due to larger atomic size and lower effective nuclear charge down the group.

Q12 (2 Marks): What happens to non-metallic character across a period? Why?

A: Non-metallic character increases because nuclear charge increases, making it easier for atoms to gain electrons.

Q13 (2 Marks): Which has greater metallic character: Sodium or Potassium? Why?

A: Potassium has greater metallic character because it is below sodium in Group 1 and can lose electrons more easily due to larger atomic size.

Q14 (2 Marks): State two reasons why hydrogen is placed separately in the periodic table.

A: 1. It resembles alkali metals by losing 1 electron.
2. It also resembles halogens by gaining 1 electron.

Q15 (2 Marks): Give the electronic configuration and group of chlorine (Atomic number 17).

A: Electronic configuration: 2,8,7; Group: 17 (Halogens).

Q1: Why did Mendeleev arrange elements in increasing order of atomic mass and not atomic number?

A: Configuration: 2,6; Period: 2; Group: 16.

A: Elements: Chlorine (Cl), Bromine (Br). Called halogens because they form salts with metals.

A: Because they have the same number of valence electrons in their outermost shells.

A: Sodium (Na) because atomic size decreases across a period due to increasing nuclear charge.

A: 1. Metals: Lose electrons easily; Non-metals: Gain electrons.

A: Phosphorus has valency 3 or 5 because its configuration (2,8,5) allows it to gain 3 electrons or share all 5 valence electrons.
