If I Were You - Notes

📖 Summary

“If I Were You” is a one-act play by Douglas James that tells the story of Gerrard, a playwright, and an intruder who enters his home with plans to kill him and assume his identity. Gerrard, with his wit and intelligence, outsmarts the intruder by convincing him that Gerrard himself is a criminal constantly on the run. Gerrard traps the intruder in a cupboard and calls the police, showing how presence of mind and calmness can turn a dangerous situation around.

📑 Important Questions & Answers

Short Answer Questions (2 Marks)

Q1: Who is Gerrard?

A1: Gerrard is a playwright who lives in a secluded house.

Q2: Who is the intruder and why does he break into Gerrard’s house?

A2: The intruder is a criminal who plans to kill Gerrard and hide by assuming his identity.

Q3: How does Gerrard describe himself to the intruder?

A3: Gerrard claims he is a criminal and a murderer constantly escaping the law.

Q4: Why does the intruder want to impersonate Gerrard?

A4: Because Gerrard lives alone and his disappearance would not attract attention.

Q5: How does Gerrard trap the intruder?

A5: He tricks the intruder into believing the cupboard is a safe exit and locks him inside.

Q6: What qualities of Gerrard help him outsmart the intruder?

A6: Calmness, intelligence, quick thinking, and presence of mind.

Q7: What does Gerrard do after locking the intruder?

A7: He calls the police to hand over the intruder.

Q8: How does the play end?

A8: The intruder is locked in and Gerrard prepares to hand him over to the authorities.

Q9: What message does the play convey?

A9: It shows the importance of wit, courage, and presence of mind in tackling dangerous situations.

Q10: Why is the title “If I Were You” appropriate?

A10: It reflects the intruder’s wish to live as Gerrard and Gerrard’s clever reversal of roles.

Long Answer Questions (5 Marks)

Q1: How does Gerrard turn the tables on the intruder?

A1: Gerrard remains calm and uses his intelligence to make the intruder believe he is a criminal. He pretends to assist but cleverly locks him in a cupboard and calls the police.

Q2: Compare the personalities of Gerrard and the intruder.

A2: Gerrard is calm, witty, and resourceful, while the intruder is overconfident, aggressive, and underestimates Gerrard.

Q3: What lesson can we learn from Gerrard’s behavior?

A3: Staying calm and thinking clearly in stressful situations can save lives and resolve crises effectively.

Q4: Why does Gerrard live in isolation? How does it help him deal with the intruder?

A4: Gerrard lives in isolation due to his work as a playwright. His solitary lifestyle and secretive nature help him convince the intruder of his false criminal background.

Q5: Write a diary entry as Gerrard after the incident.

A5: *“Dear Diary, Tonight I used my wit to outsmart a dangerous intruder. It was frightening, but I remained calm. I’m proud that I prevented a crime and stayed safe.”*

📌 Key Takeaways

  • 🧠 Presence of mind can turn the tables in dangerous situations.
  • 😎 Wit and quick thinking are powerful tools against threats.
  • 📖 The play blends humor with suspense and a moral lesson.
